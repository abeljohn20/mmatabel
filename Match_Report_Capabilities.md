# What You Get From a Match Report

## 1. Shot-by-Shot Breakdown with Court Mapping

Every shot you played in the match is classified, measured, and mapped to exact court zones. You see which shots are your **weapons** (high effectiveness) and which are **liabilities** (costing you points). Each shot type shows effectiveness percentage, frequency, accuracy, and height/length variations. An interactive 3D court lets you tap any zone to see what you hit from there, where it landed, and how effective it was. You can compare your shot distribution against your opponent's side-by-side.

## 2. Opening Phase Strategy Analysis

Your serve and receive game is dissected to reveal predictability and effectiveness patterns. The report flags if your serve is too predictable (e.g., "90% to the middle"), identifies your **best and worst receive shots** with video evidence, and maps out how your opponent responds to each serve type. Winning and losing opening sequences are extracted as step-by-step patterns (e.g., You Serve -> Opp Reset -> You Attack) with effectiveness at each step, so you know exactly which openings to repeat and which to avoid.

## 3. Tactical Patterns, Tempo, and Predictability

Rally length analysis shows whether you dominate short, medium, or long rallies, with win rates for each. Match phase tracking reveals how your effectiveness changes across the Start, Pre-interval, Championship, and End-game phases -- exposing when you fade under pressure. Tempo control identifies which shots speed up or slow down play and whether that helps or hurts you. A predictability score flags your most repetitive responses (e.g., "After opponent's pulldrop, you lift 50% of the time") that opponents can exploit, along with winning and losing sequence patterns.

## 4. Decision Quality and Pressure Performance

The report evaluates your **shot selection** by comparing what you played against better options available in each court position (e.g., "Backhand clear at 26% effectiveness when overhead clear at 51% was available"). **Tipping points** identify which shots become weapons or liabilities under pressure -- some shots jump from 62% to 91% effectiveness in tight moments while others collapse from 100% to 45%. Post-shot recovery analysis shows which shots leave you in good or bad positions, and adaptive performance tracks how your effectiveness shifts across different game phases.

## 5. Head-to-Head Dynamics and Momentum

Game-by-game **scoring streaks** are visualized as an interactive timeline showing who had momentum and when it shifted, with video playback for each run. Leading vs trailing analysis reveals whether you perform better when chasing (many players do) or when protecting a lead. Category-by-category stat comparisons show where you outperformed your opponent and where they had the edge. Winners and unforced errors are tallied for both sides, and a **play style classification** (Attacking, Balanced, Defensive, Net Player) characterizes how each player approached the match with narrative explanation.
